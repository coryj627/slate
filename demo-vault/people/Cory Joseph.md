# Cory Joseph

Digital Accessibility Architect, based in Parma, Ohio. Professional background spans Intel, Microsoft, and Amazon, with most of the work focused on enterprise accessibility standards, assistive-technology integration, and the slow institutional work of making large engineering organizations actually ship accessible software rather than just claim to.

Cory uses VoiceOver and JAWS as primary assistive technologies, navigates entirely by keyboard, and writes from a perspective grounded in firsthand experience rather than received best-practice. His blog at startingblind.com covers disability inclusion, accessibility, and the gap between accessible-on-paper and usable-in-practice.

Outside the day job: a weekly D&D group, a Proxmox home lab named `moxy`, and an ongoing project to build [[Slate]] — the notes app this vault exists to test.
