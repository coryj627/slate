# Apple pie

A working recipe for a 9-inch double-crust apple pie. Not fancy. The crust is a basic all-butter pie dough; the filling is granny smith apples with the conventional cinnamon-sugar treatment. The serving suggestion is whipped cream — recipe at [[Whipped cream]], and the whole thing is embedded at the end of this note for convenience.

![A simple line drawing of a pie with one slice removed, used here as the recipe illustration.](attachments/pie.svg)

## Ingredients

For the crust:

- 2½ cups all-purpose flour
- 1 tsp salt
- 1 tbsp sugar
- 1 cup (2 sticks) unsalted butter, very cold, cubed
- 6–8 tbsp ice water

For the filling:

- 2½ lb granny smith apples (about 6 medium), peeled, cored, sliced ¼-inch thick
- ¾ cup sugar
- 2 tbsp flour
- 1 tbsp lemon juice
- 1 tsp cinnamon
- ¼ tsp salt
- 1 tbsp butter, cut into small pieces

## Method

1. Make the crust. Whisk flour, salt, and sugar in a large bowl. Cut in the butter with a pastry blender or your fingertips until the mixture looks like coarse meal with some pea-sized pieces still visible. Add ice water a tablespoon at a time, mixing gently, until the dough just holds together when pressed.
2. Divide the dough in two, flatten each half into a disc, wrap, and chill at least 1 hour.
3. Make the filling. Toss the sliced apples with the sugar, flour, lemon juice, cinnamon, and salt. Let sit 15 minutes; the apples will release some liquid, which is correct.
4. Preheat oven to 425°F (220°C).
5. Roll out the bottom crust, fit it into a 9-inch pie pan, and trim the overhang to about ½ inch. Mound the filling into the shell and dot with the small butter pieces.
6. Roll out the top crust, lay it over the filling, trim, and crimp the edges. Cut 4–5 steam vents in the top.
7. Bake at 425°F for 20 minutes, then reduce to 375°F (190°C) and bake another 35–45 minutes, until the crust is deep golden brown and the filling bubbles visibly through the vents.
8. Cool on a rack at least 2 hours before slicing. This step is non-negotiable — slicing too early gives you apple soup.

## Serving

Serve warm or at room temperature, with a generous spoonful of whipped cream. The cream stabilizes after step 2 of the whipped-cream method — see [[Whipped cream#^method-step-2]] for the exact texture target — and holds in the fridge for a few hours.
