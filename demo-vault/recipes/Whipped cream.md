# Whipped cream

The simplest possible whipped cream. Three ingredients, one bowl, five minutes. Used as the topping for [[Apple pie]] and approximately everything else.

## Ingredients

- 1 cup heavy whipping cream, cold
- 2 tbsp powdered sugar (or granulated, if that's what you have)
- ½ tsp vanilla extract

## Method

1. Chill the bowl and the whisk (or the bowl and beaters) in the freezer for 10 minutes. Cold equipment makes the cream whip faster and hold its shape longer.
2. Pour the cream into the chilled bowl. Whisk on medium-high until soft peaks form — the cream should hold a peak that just barely curls back on itself when you lift the whisk. Stop here for serving over warm desserts; continue one more notch to medium peaks if you want it to hold longer or be piped. ^method-step-2
3. Add the powdered sugar and vanilla. Whisk just until incorporated, no more than 10 or 15 seconds. Overbeating past this point breaks the cream into butter, which is recoverable for cooking but not for serving.
4. Serve immediately, or hold in the refrigerator for up to a few hours. Re-whisk gently if it slackens.

## Notes

If the cream is breaking and starting to look grainy, you've gone too far. Add a splash of fresh cold cream and whisk briefly to bring it back; this works once but not twice. Beyond that, accept it and use the result as a butter substitute somewhere.
